Planning a Trip?

Use the 'Travel' tag plus your destination to find others who have been there. Ask questions and read review from people you can trust.

Take it to another level and add the specific dates you are traveling. This will bring up an entirely new group of everyone else who will be vacationing to the same desitination. From there you can plan ahead and meet a travel buddy!

Attending a concert/festival?

Weekend festivals can be the best weekend of you life, or the worst. It all depends on how prepared you are.

Our playfrom allows first-time festival goers to get in touch with experts and ask questions. Additionally it connects everyone who is going to the next weekend concert allowing them to coordinate on things such as food, water and camping gear.

Personal cases where our founders would have used root if it was available

Series 7 Course

I attended a Series 7 course with many other students to prep for our upcoming test. Our class was very close and interactive - we studied together, shares notes and helped eachother out.

At the end of the course, I was scheduled to take the exam that upcoming Friday which was the first in the class. All of my classmates wished me luck and told me to let them know how I did and give them pointers.

I know that there was no way to reach back out to let my classmates know the results and what to focus on. Root would provide a platfrom that would connect our class during the class, and act as a way to stay in touch.

Adult League Sports

Every Tuesday, I play in an adult hockey league. This inevitable means that on Tuesday's at around 2PM, I am bombarded with annoying emails - first from the team manager, then from each player letting everyone know if they're IN or OUT for the game.

Every week it's the same. The simple IN or OUT turns into a friendly trash-talking email chain between teammates. For the remainder of the afernoon, I get emails about how bad Steve was last week and how he "couldn't finish a hooker."

This being the 21st century and millenial, I am used to constant notifications. The only problem is when I go back to look at the chain, it is confusing and out of order. I can't tell who send what and when. Additionally, who the heck is 'hockey4lyfe@aol.com'?.

Root would let us take our team email chain to a new, simple yet more orgaized platform. A new chat could be started each week that functions as seamless as a groupchat. Furthermore, each player has their real name and profile pic so you'll never forget which Steve sucks at hockey.
